Cloud computing started from early network schematics as the computers connected to servers drawn could all be linked but when too many servers going over each other made a symbol of a cloud allowing for cloud computing to start. By 1996, cloud computing had been changed into cloud services, making it Software as a Service (SaaS), Platform as a Service (PaaS), and Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS). In 2003, an amazon development team from South Africa presented a new idea, this was to make a system that was made of mostly web-based services. This would have been also fully automated which would require the least amount of system maintenance that other developers could use, and this idea became successful. This was where the growth of cloud computing started to spread and soon all types of companies started using this which increased their speed and reliability by a whole lot. This Amazon Web Services started being used for backing up data, recovering loss of data, software development and testing, and data analytics.

As an example, cloud computing is used by companies so they do not have to buy their own data centers, they can buy virtual cloud service providers for rent so they can access apps to storage. This allows them to not have to put more cost into managing the centers or infrastructures that are bought but just rent virtual servers or services. Further into software as a service which can include customer relationship management, emails, virtual desktops, communication, and video games. Platform as a service can include e-commerce tools, databases, web servers, and development tools as examples. Infrastructure as a service can include virtual machines, servers, storage, load balancers, and networks as some examples. Cloud is another way of saying that any number of devices can access these services through the use of the internet.

Cybersecurity is a digital method of protecting systems, networks and any programs from potential cyber threats. These cyber-attacks come in many forms which include, malware, ransomware, phishing attacks, man-in-the-middle and many more. It helps keep these systems safe and contained, therefore preventing any digital attacks and infiltration of any sort of these systems. It is essential for important systems used in the government, military, major companies or corporate businesses in order to maintain data integrity and prevent any sort of cyber threats or attacks.

As technology evolves and is constantly advancing, there are new ways to hack or attack systems every day, ultimately increasing the importance of cybersecurity. However because of this, cybersecurity was also able to improve and obtain heavier and more secure protection. Due to the constant increase of cyber-attacks, organisations all over the world have turned to human-centric cybersecurity. This form of cybersecurity allows humans to take control more than machines or programs to protect their systems. By having humans having a higher percentage of participation and control over the security of these systems, they are able to gain a deeper understanding of how users interact with the data and the possible risks that are the outcome of these interactions. As humans are the users and the creators of our technologies and systems, humans have the ability to approach situations with a deeper understanding and knowledge of these potential cyber-attacks and threats.

The most common uses of cybersecurity known today are the prevention of viruses in our computer systems or even the prevention of hackers through unsecure passwords for our online accounts. There have been many new methods in which can prevent any potential hackers from attacking these accounts through simple 2 step authentications or even just more complex passwords with symbols, numbers and different case letters embedded.

According to chief security scientists, ransomware will be emerging in larger numbers and execute more threats for the next few years. As ransomware is constantly evolving along with today’s technology, the attacks and threats will therefore be more difficult to detect and counterattack. Ransomware attacks are especially important to be eliminated as they target all user groups such as any companies, the governments, hospitals and many more. A new and arising development for aiding the protection of any sort of systems is the use of quantum technology and how it affects the encryption methods we use. This advancement will possibly be evident more in the near future as encryption still requires more exploration and understanding of its potential use to its fullest capacity. Encryption is also another emerging method of cybersecurity known for its efficiency in protecting data as simple as texts, emails and bank information.

There is a huge variety of technologies and software available for use for the average person which provide protection for endpoint devices such as computers, mobiles, routers, networks and the cloud. As these all may be present in any individual’s life, there are many technologies and software’s to choose to provide protection from unwanted external users. Common technology used for these purposes include next-generation firewalls, DNS filtering, antivirus software’s,

Context-Aware Behavioural Analytics, Virtual Dispersive Networking (VDN), malware protection just to name a few. Context-Aware Behavioural Analytics is a common form of cybersecurity mainly for larger organisations. It uses sophisticated behavioural analytics to supervise and identify any apprehensive interactions and transactions towards their data and systems. This type of security is more efficient than static infrastructure security such as firewalls and antivirus software which do not possess the highly advanced and sensitive data of the world of technology today. Another standard form of cybersecurity is Virtual Dispersive Networking (VDN) which is mainly utilised to counterattack MIM (man-in-the-middle) attacks. This allows this method to actively seek the attacker who is essentially breaking through the systems from internally to the outside, interrupting the communication between the sender and receiver and its pathway. VDN are highly valued in large companies or government settings due to its ability to maintain data integrity as they are associated with commercial industries with high-value targets such as banks and utilities. Therefore are replacing Virtual Private Networks (VPN’s) as VDN’s provide greater security and infrastructure allowing a smoother management and thereby performance.

Blockchain and cryptocurrencies

What does it do? What is the state of the art of this new technology? What can be done now? What is likely to be able to do be done soon (say in the next 3 years)? What technological or other developments make this possible?

A block chain stores and structures crypto currency transactions, legal contracts and inventory products into chunks, otherwise known as blocks, unlike most database which store data in tables with rows and columns, blockchain forms a chainlike formation. These blocks are all connected to one another and allow for crypto/ online currencies to be transferred legitimately and successfully between users. For example, bitcoin requires a set of computers to store its blockchain, these blockchains store every bitcoin transaction that have occurred throughout its history. What makes bitcoins database unique is that its computers are not under one roof, instead each group of computers or single computers are operated by a unique individual or individuals. These computers that make up bitcoins network are called nodes. In a blockchain, each node has a full record of the data that has been stored on the blockchain since its inception. For Bitcoin, the data is the entire history of all Bitcoin transactions. If one node has an error in its data it can use many other nodes as a reference point to correct itself. This way, no one node of the network can alter information held within it. Because of this, the history of transactions in each block that make up Bitcoin’s blockchain cannot be reversed.

Block chains seemingly tamper proof design saw it become heralded as the next big thing in emerging technology a couple of years ago. A study done by statista revealed that 33% of global organisations have said that their companies are working on creating a digital currency using blockchain resulting in an increase from $3 billion in 2020 to $39 billion by 2025. This data shows how much of an impact block chain will have on the world in the next few years. Despite the future benefits, blockchain is also currently being used in several other ways which include payment processing and money transfers, monetization of supply chains, digital IDs, copyright and royalty protection and many more. Crypto currency is another major technology that has been protected and managed with the help of block chain, this has allowed crypto currencies like bitcoin grow exponentially in the past 5 years. As crypto currencies grow they provide many advantages to their owners, one benefit includes a lower transaction fee. When you check the bank statement, you would be cribbing on the transaction fee charged for every transaction you are carrying out. If you are entrusting the responsibility of maintaining your crypto wallet to the third party, you must pay for the service. However, the transaction fees charged by cryptocurrency transactions would be lesser compared to the traditional financial system.

The main way blockchain is maintained is through a process called mining, this is where miners create new blocks on the chain by making a unique hash and nonce. This process is very complex and difficult but It ultimately allows for block chain to be a secure and safe technology.

In finance, CPA auditors audit and help multi trillion dollar capital markets system function with high confidence. CPA auditors practice under strict regulations, professional codes of conduct and auditing standards, and are independent of the entities they audit. They apply objectivity and professional scepticism to provide reasonable assurance about whether an entity’s financial statements are free of material misstatement and, depending on the engagement, about whether a company’s internal controls over financial reporting are operating effectively. Some publications have signalled that blockchain technology may eliminate the need for a financial statement audit by a CPA auditor altogether. If all transactions are captured in an immutable blockchain, there will not be much left for the CPA auditor to edit. Also, a blockchain that records the transfer of a particular currency may make it difficult for the CPA auditor to determine what the product was solely by looking at the block chain. Therefore a transaction recorded in a block chain may still be unauthorised, fraudulent or illegal and incorrectly classified in financial statements.
